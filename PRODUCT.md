# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two overlapping audiences who find each other in the same place:

- **Aesthetes and hobbyists** who discovered the app through its newspaper look and stay because it feels calm and unhurried compared to gamer-coded typing tests. They type for the atmosphere, not the leaderboard.
- **Skill builders** who want to practice with real, meaningful content (daily news) rather than random word lists or pangrams. The newspaper format gives their practice a reason to exist.

Context: alone, at a desk, probably with some ambient noise. Not a competitive session. More like a morning ritual.

## Product Purpose

A vintage newspaper-themed typing game that serves fresh daily news as the typing content. Success looks like a user finishing an article feeling like they read *and* typed something worthwhile, with clean stats to show for it. The newspaper frame is not decoration — it is the content delivery system.

## Brand Personality

Print-authoritative, unhurried, tasteful.

Emotional goal: the feeling of reading a real newspaper at a cafe — absorbed, unhurried, a little nostalgic. When typing fast, it should feel like rhythm, not racing.

## Anti-references

- **Monkeytype / Typeracer as-is** — generic dark mode with neon accents, gamer HUD energy, purely metric-obsessed.
- **SaaS dashboard pattern** — cards, progress rings, metric widgets, corporate blue-grey palette. No data-heavy chrome.
- **Wordle-style flat pastels** — playful and colorful in a way that shatters the newspaper illusion.
- **Figma / Notion neutral** — clean but characterless. Too beige, no distinct point of view.

## Design Principles

1. **The text is the product.** The typing surface is sacred. Nothing competes with it visually or hierarchically.
2. **Print-born, screen-adapted.** Every decision should feel like it was designed for paper first. Column structure, ink-weight typography, hard contrast — not faked with textures, but built into the layout.
3. **Earned feedback.** Stats appear when they serve the user, then recede. Never billboard metrics above the content.
4. **Authority, not pastiche.** Reference the *substance* of print journalism (hierarchy, type scale, grid discipline), not its decoration (distressed edges, sepia washes, ornamental borders).
5. **Rhythm over speed.** The experience should feel like reading, not racing. Mechanical feedback on correct keys, atmospheric quiet on correct streaks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Keyboard navigation is inherent to the product (it is a typing game). Ensure focus states are visible and distinct. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations (cursor blink, toast slide-in).
